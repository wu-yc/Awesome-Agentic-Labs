üåü Overview  
Agentic labs represent a transformative era in AI-driven science, where autonomous systems powered by large language models (LLMs), multimodal capabilities, and integrated platforms function as full research partners. These agents generate hypotheses, design and execute experiments, analyze data, and iteratively refine processes across domains like life sciences, chemistry, materials science, physics, and astronomy. Research indicates this evolution from AI-assisted tools to agentic autonomy could accelerate discoveries by factors of 10-100, though challenges in reproducibility, ethical governance, and human oversight persist. Evidence suggests agentic infrastructures may democratize science, but equitable access and balanced human-AI collaboration are essential. For foundational insights, see surveys on autonomous discovery and agentic frameworks.

Agentic labs signify a shift where AI infrastructures enable independent research cycles, accelerating discoveries across disciplines. This overview synthesizes foundational works, frameworks, and applications from surveys and repositories, offering a self-contained resource.

### Evolution of AI in Science
AI has advanced from aids to agentic systems with autonomy levels from pattern recognition (Level 1) to generative hypothesis invention (Level 3-4). Sci-LLMs like Galactica specialize in reasoning, integrating multimodal data.

### Core Capabilities
- **Planning and Reasoning**: CoT, ToT, ReAct for decision-making.
- **Tool Integration**: Databases, simulators, robotics.
- **Memory**: Short/long-term for learning.
- **Multi-Agent**: Hierarchical/debate for refinement.
- **Optimization**: Self-reward RL, feedback.

Challenges: Hallucination, reliability, ethics.

### Workflow Stages
1. Hypothesis Generation: Literature mining.
2. Experimental Planning: Tool orchestration.
3. Data Analysis: Validation.
4. Synthesis: Refinement.

### Domain Applications
#### Life Sciences
Agents automate genomics, protein engineering, drug discovery. E.g., CellAgent achieves expert insights in scRNA-seq.

|------------|----------|----------|----------|

Controversies: Privacy, bias.

Tool-augmented synthesis, generative design. E.g., ChemCrow executes reactions.
